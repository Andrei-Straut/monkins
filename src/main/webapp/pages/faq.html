Jenkins Monitor FAQ

What's the significance of the colors?

Color codes follow the Jenkins conventions, meaning:
  • Green is for successful jobs (jobs that have completed successfully, or, in the case of test jobs, jobs who have completed successfully with tests passed)
  • Yellow is for jobs that are currently unstable
  • Red is for jobs that have failed, whether due to job errors, or test failures

Why are some jobs' titles fading in and out?

Job titles fading in and out means that the job is currently running. Background color is the status of the last completed build, and becomes automatically updated upon job completion.

Why are some jobs white?

White is undefined (default) status. If a job's color is white, is because there was an error retrieving its data.

Why in some cases I correctly see Last Successful, Last Failed and Test Results data, and in others not?

By default, these details are only reported for failed or unstable builds, as there is no immediate necessity to always check details for a build that is going well.

Also, this depends on how your Jenkins reports data. Things may vary due to versions, or in the case of Last Successful or Last Failed fields, the data may simply not be available (i.e. there was never a successful or failed build). Absence of test result data is particularly common for build jobs, who may simply execute the build process, without running tests in and of themselves.
